About

Early Life

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (P. V. Sindhu) was born on 5 July 1995 to P. V. Ramana and P. Vijaya in Hyderabad, Telangana. Both of her parents were professional volleyball players. Sindhu has an elder sister, P. V. Divya, who works as a doctor and Sindhu is the youngest one in the family.

Sindhu was very interested in playing badminton. When she was just eight years old, she realised her passion for playing badminton. As both of Sindhu’s parents were sportspersons, they wholeheartedly supported her to make a career in badminton. she drew inspiration from the success of Pullela Gopichand, the 2001 All England Open Badminton Champion.

The Never-Say-Die Spirit

In 2004, P. V. Sindhu joined Gopichand Badminton Academy, where she received training from her idol, Gopichand. Sindhu used to wake up daily at 3:30 AM in the early morning and travel a distance of 56 kilometres as a pillion rider on her father’s scooter from her home to reach the Gopichand Badminton Academy.And she used to start her badminton practice sessions at 4:30 AM.

After completing the session, Sindhu used to reach the school at 8:30 AM. And after finishing her school at 3:30 PM, she used to reach the Gopichand Badminton Academy at 4:30 PM and used to start practising badminton. This routine continued for four years.

Sindhu simultaneously managed her studies along with playing badminton and she never neglected her studies.

Gopichand once said "the most striking feature in Pusarla's game is her attitude and the never-say-die spirit."

Achievements

2009

  • Entered the international circuit at a young age of 14
  • Bronze medallist at the 2009 Sub-Junior Asian Badminton Championships

2010

  • Won the silver medal in Iran Fajr International Badminton Challenge
  • Quarterfinals of the 2010 BWF World Junior Championships

2011

  • Won Maldives International Challenge
  • Indonesia International Challenge
  • Swiss International
  • India International badminton

2012

  • Won Asian Junior Championships
  • Semi-finalist in London 2012 Olympics

2013

  • Reached her career best ranking of 15
  • Was awarded the Arjun Award by Government of India
  • Won Macau Open Grand Prix Gold title

2014

  • Claimed her first medal at the Asian Championships
  • Bronze medal in 2014 Commonwealth Games

2015

  • won her third successive women's singles title at the Macau Open Grand Prix Gold

2016

  • Sindhu won a silver medal at the 2016 Summer Olympics hosted at Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
  • She created history by becoming the First Indian Woman to Win a Silver Medal in badminton.

2017

  • Silver medal in BWF World Championships

2018

  • Won gold medal in 2018 Commonwealth Games in the mixed team event
  • Silver medal in singles event

2019

  • On 25 August 2019 she won a gold medal in the World Championships held at Basel in Switzerland.
  • It took her just 38 minutes to win the match and create history
  • She became the First Indian to Win Gold at the World Championships.
